Fintech Terminology

Blockchain

Blockchain is a type of distributed ledger. It requires entries to be confirmed and encrypted by an advanced cryptography. This makes the entries very difficult to change or hack.

Cryptocurrency

An asset of a decentralized network that can be traded and used as as a medium of exchange, and used as a store of value. 

Digital Asset

Non-tangible asset is one that can be created, traded, and stored in a digital format. In blockchains, digital assets include cryptocurrency and tokens.

Decentralized Finance

Decentralized Finance is finance not dependent on central financial exchanges, or banks. It can be used with blockchains for financial functions and depend on the code of smart contracts.

Fiat Currency 

Sometimes called "real money", fiat currency is government-issued and not backed by a commodity, such as gold.

Mining

A process by which some digital assets, are entered into circulation. This validates and adds transactions to the blockchain and brings digital assets into circulation.

Peer-to-peer

A decentralized system whereby two persons interact with each other without a third-party in between. 

Public Key

The code for your digital wallet. The public key is shared with others so they can send digital assets.

Private Key

A personal code which allows access to a digital wallet. Without it, the wallet and related digital assets cannot be accessed (even by the owner).

Smart contracts

Programs stored on a blockchain that run when certain conditions are met. 

Tokens

Tokens are used in blockchain networks. They are built on top of those blockchains.

Virtual currency

Virtual currency is a digital medium of exchange.

Wallet

A digital wallet is an app that keeps track of personal finances and budgeting.

Purchase-to-pay

Purchase-to-pay, (P2P), can cover things like, purchasing, receiving and paying for items and services. They are automated processes that reduce labor costs.
